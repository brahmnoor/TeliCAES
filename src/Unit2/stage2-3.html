Stage 2-3 Examples of linking words

Note that there are some overlaps with prepositions and adverbial expressions, but this section will focus on their usage as linking words.

    Purpose of Expression Examples of Linking Words The reader will...
    Examples Also, again, moreover, additionally, in the same way, furthermore, comparatively, as well as, together with, not only... but also, and Look for additional information about the preceding idea.
    Similarity Similarly, likewise, too, as, like, in like manner Look for an example of ideas that matches the preceding ones.
    Contradiction / Limitation But, however, rather, nevertheless, nonetheless, regardless, even though, although, despite, unlike, yet, while, albeit, besides, in contrast, on the other hand, on the contrary, in spite of Expect a change of direction in arguments, or see how presented ideas differ.
    Cause / Condition If, ...then, provided that, in the event that, unless, as long as, on the condition that, given that, due to, because of, since, while, in order to, seeing / being that, in view of, with the intention of Pay attention to the specific conditions or intentions.
    Effect / Consequence Consequently, therefore, accordingly, henceforth, as a result, under these circumstances, thus, for, because of, then, hence, for this reason, in effect Link cause and effect, and try to understand their relations
    Examples / Clarification In other words, including, for instance, for example, as an illustration, that is to say, apparently, to put it another way, to point out, to clarify, to repeat, to demonstrate, such as Look for specific examples that illustrate the preceding statement.
    Emphasis Indeed, in fact, notably, in particular, especially, specifically, surprisingly, must be reminded that, Pay special attention to what the author wants to highlight.
    Summary To sum up, in short, in brief, as can be seen, all things considered, as shown above, given these points, as has been noted, in conclusion, to summarize, on the whole Expect authorâ€™s final statement of the main discussion
    Sequence At the same time, as soon as, in the meantime, at this instant, eventually, followed by, earlier, later, until, further, during, prior to, by the time, immediately, suddenly, after, before, hence, since, once, next, now Expect to follow information in logical order.
