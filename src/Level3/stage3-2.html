Stage 3-2 Reference Agreement

A pronoun must agree with its antecedent (i.e. the noun to which the pronoun refers) in terms of number and person, and the relationship between the two must be clear and unambiguous. A pronoun with multiple, possible antecedents would confuse a reader.

    Ambiguous Pronoun References:
  • Error: When Tom dropped the vase onto the table, it broke. (What broke? The vase or the table?)
  • Correction: The vase broke when Tom dropped it onto the table.

  • Error: Phoebe had an internship in a law firm last summer; this will become her career. (What does “this” refer to?)
  • Correction: Phoebe had an internship in a law firm last summer; this work will become her career.
    Vague Pronoun References:
  • Error: They should increase funding to academic programs. (Who is “they”? No explicit antecedent.)
  • Correction: The administration should increase funding to academic programs

  • Error: My brother is an engineer, but I’m not interested in it. (What is the antecedent of “it”?)
  • Correction: My brother is an engineer, but I’m not interested in being an engineer.
