Stage 5-3 Passive Voice and Academic Modesty

  • Sometimes, academic writers hide the agent/doer of an action to make the message sound less combative, accusative and inflammatory. Look at the example below, with the doer/agent (the Hong Kong Government) taken away. Note how the message has been “toned down” by removing the subject.

    For Example:

    1. ‘The Hong Kong Government handled the housing issues poorly.’ VS
    2. ‘It is believed that housing issues in Hong Kong were poorly handled.’

    Of course, whether one decides to be more combative or less direct when putting forward their arguments depends on a variety of factors such as the level of evidence that one has and the disciplinary variation and existing norm in presenting an argument.
