Stage 5-1 Using Active and Passive Voice in Academic Writing

When to use active and passive voices...

  • Active

    As a general rule, you should only use the passive voice when you have a good reason NOT to use the active voice. Writing is usually clearer and more interesting in the active voice - this includes academic writing.

    However, this does not mean that the passive voice should be completely avoided. Some conditions/situations warrant the use of the passive voice.

  • Passive

    As a general rule, you should only use the passive voice when you have a good reason NOT to use the active voice. Writing is usually clearer and more interesting in the active voice - this includes academic writing.

    However, this does not mean that the passive voice should be completely avoided. Some conditions/situations warrant the use of the passive voice.

    The passive voice can be used in the following situations:

    when we do not know who the subject/doer/agent is.

    when the subject/doer is very general and obvious.

    when the subject/doer is not the important information in the sentence, something else is.

    when we want to hide the subject from the reader, for some reason.
