Stage 1-2 Common errors in subject-verb agreement

Subject-verb agreement is not as complicated as other grammatical items like hedging, nominalization, tenses, etc. in academic texts. However, students sometimes find it difficult to use the correct subject-verb agreement as they might identify the subject incorrectly. The following are some common errors students might make.

  • A. When the subject is a list of items:

    Nursing, Pharmacy, Chinese Medicine, and Physiotherapy is very popular in Hong Kong.

    Nursing, Pharmacy, Chinese Medicine, and Physiotherapy are very popular in Hong Kong.

    In this example, the list ‘ Nursing, Pharmacy, Chinese Medicine, and Physiotherapy ’ is treated as separate academic disciplines; as a result, a plural verb is necessary.
  • B. When the subject is a collective noun:

    A group of Hong Kong students win the championship.

    A group of Hong Kong students wins the championship.

    In the above example, the subject is a collective noun ‘ a group of ’ A singular verb should be used. When you use other collective nouns such as ‘ a crowd of ’, ‘ a collection of ’, ‘ a herd of ’,etc., a singular verb should be chosen.
  • C. When the subject is separated from the verb by a long phrase/clause:

    Johnny, one of my best friends, play piano daily as he wants to be a great pianist.

    Johnny, one of my best friends, plays piano daily as he wants to be a great pianist.

    In the above example, students usually mistakenly use the verb ‘ play ’ as they see the word ‘ friends ’ right in front of the verb. However, the subject here is separated from the verb by a long phrase. As a result, the subject is ‘ Johnny ’ instead of ‘ friends ’. We must be very careful when dealing with the subject separated from the verb by a long phrase/clause.
  • D. When the subject is separated by phrases headed with as well as, together with, and along with:

    That professor, as well as her research assistant, are about to give a speech to everyone.

    That professor, as well as her research assistant, is about to give a speech to everyone.

    That professor, together with her colleagues, are about to give a presentation to the students.

    That professor, together with her colleagues, is about to give a presentation to the students.

    When the subject is separated by phrases headed with as well as, together with, and along with, these phrases should be ignored and a singular verb should be used if the subject is a singular noun phrase.
  • E. When the subject is in the form of either…or / neither…nor construction:

    Either research method A or research method B are inappropriate.

    Either research method A or research method B is inappropriate.

    Neither research method A nor research method B are appropriate.

    Neither research method A nor research method B is appropriate.

    When the two singular noun phrases in the subject position are linked by the either…or / neither…nor construction, a singular verb should be used.

    Either one medium-sized container or two small-sized ones is given to the research assistants.

    Either one medium-sized container or two small-sized ones are given to the research assistants.

    Either two small-sized containers or a medium-sized one are are given to the research assistants.

    Either two small-sized containers or a medium-sized one are is given to the research assistants.

    If there are two noun phrases (linked by the either…or / neither…nor construction) in the subject position, the one closer to the verb will determine its number.
  • F. When the subject is a unit of time, money, or distance:

    Ten years are needed before a researcher can ascertain the safety of a new drug.

    Ten years is needed before a researcher can ascertain the safety of a new drug.

    US$5 billion dollars were allocated to cancer research last year.

    US$5 billion dollars was allocated to cancer research last year.

    Ten kilometers are a very short distance to any professional marathon runners.

    Ten kilometers are a very short distance to any professional marathon runners.

    Whenever the subject is a unit of time, money, or distance, a singular verb should be used.
