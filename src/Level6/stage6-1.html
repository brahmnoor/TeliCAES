Stage 6-1 Hedging, Boosting and Balancing Your Argument

Why Should You Use “Cautious Language”?

Both hedging and boosting are effective tools for balancing argument and making accurate claims in academic writing and speaking.

Hedging can be used as a “polite strategy” to soften your speech. On the other hand, functions of hedging are more complicated in writing. Compare the examples below:

  • Speaking:

    1. You should apply to all universities in Hong Kong (direct).

    2. Perhaps you may apply to all universities in Hong Kong (less direct).

  • Writing:

    1. The growing class size means that university students will have less face-to-face time with their teachers (assertive).

    2. The growing class size seems to imply that university students will have less face-to-face time with their teachers (Shows some doubts? Acknowledges opposing viewpoint? Or the writer does not have enough evidence to make an assertive claim?).
