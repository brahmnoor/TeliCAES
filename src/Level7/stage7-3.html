Stage 7-3 Be Meticulous in Using Nominalization in Academic Writing

  • It has to be noted that if nominalization is overused, clear and effective communication will be severely obstructed. It is strongly advised that nominalization should be used with extra caution. Read the two problematic examples below demonstrating the excessive (or extreme) use of nominalization and their suggested revision:

    Problematic Cases of Nominalization ➔ Revised Version
    1. The proliferation of nominalizations in a discursive formation may be an indication of a tendency towards pomposity and abstraction (Sword, 2012). Writers who overload their sentences with nominalizations tend to sound pompous and abstract.
    2. Objective considerations of contemporary phenomena compel the conclusion that success or failure in competitive activities exhibits no tendency to be commensurate with innate capacity, but that a considerable element of the unpredictable must invariably be taken into account (Orwell, 1946). It can be reasonably concluded that success or failure of an activity depends not so much on one’s innate capacity, but largely on random factors.

    In both Examples 1 and 2 above, there are seven and ten instances of nominalizations respectively. With the overloading of all the nominal phrases, the genuine meanings of the two sentences are totally obscure and readers will most likely be baffled by them.
