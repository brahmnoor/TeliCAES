  • The effect of violent video games on aggression: Is it more than just the violence?
    The first study conducted into the effect of watching violent videos, over ten years ago, showed no link to aggressive behaviour. However recent experimental research has shown that playing violent video games leads to higher levels of aggressive behavior (in the short-term) than non-violent video games. There are two major limitations with these investigations, however. First, the majority of experimental studies that have compared the effects of violent versus non-violent video games on aggression have failed to equate these games in terms of competitiveness, difficulty, and pace of action. Thus, it seems that violent video games produce higher levels of aggression than non-violent video games, other factors beyond the actual violent content are possibly responsible for the elevated levels of aggression. Second, previous experimental studies have usually used a measure of aggression that also measures competitiveness, leading to questions about whether violent video games are related to aggression or competitiveness.
