Stage 4-7 Tips on Choosing Tenses Academic Writing

Present Simple Tense
Used for:

  • 1. Introducing a topic with a general statement
  • 2. Making statements about what is currently true
  • 3. Referring to existing research relevant to your work
  • 4. Explaining the significance of the results in the discussion

Past Simple Tense
Used for:

  • 1. Reporting another's completed research
  • 2. Describing methods and data
  • 3. Distancing yourself from a study's finding to show disagreement

Present Perfect Tense
Used for:

  • 1. Indicating a gap in the previous research
  • 2. Bridging previous related research to yours
