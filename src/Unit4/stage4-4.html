Stage 4-4 Past Simple Tense

    Past Simple Tense is generally used for the following purposes:
  • 1. To bring other studies into your text, especially those which are relatively dated:

    e.g. Penicillin was considered the drug of choice in the treatment of anaerobic infections (Tally, Stutter & Finegold, 1975).

  • 2. In many cases, the simple past is used to as a "distancing" device to report findings/results/opinions that an author deems false or does not agree with:

    e.g. “As long as the researcher used language skillfully and avoided personal bias, then the reader could simply decode the transmitted message to recover the same reality. In contrast to this Shannon and Weaver' s (1963) view of communication, a great deal of research has recently shown how professional writers seek to achieve successful interaction with their readers while maintaining the integrity of their data” (Hyland, 2001, pp.207-208).

  • 3. To describe what you did in your study. In the results section, the past tense is commonly used to report your findings. However, you may need to check first if this is the case in your field. Notice the use of passive voice in the examples below.

    e.g. The interviews were conducted with experienced researchers from the target disciplines using a structured format (Hyland, 2001). (Humanities)

    e.g. An established protocol was observed for the briefing of the participants. (Science)

    e.g. Results indicated that extended exposure to sunlight had a strong correlation with the one's possibility of developing skin cancer. (Science)

    e.g. The control group of students took the course previously. (Education)
